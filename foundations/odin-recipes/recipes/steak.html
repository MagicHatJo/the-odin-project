Pan-Seared Garlic Butter Steak

This juicy, tender steak is perfect for garlic lovers. This recipe makes for a wonderful weeknight dinner.

Published September 24, 2023 by Chef MeowMeow

A mouthwatering dish that elevates the humble steak into a culinary masterpiece. A succulent cut of steak is seared to perfection, then topped with a luscious garlic butter sauce that's rich, fragrant, and bursting with flavor. This combination of tender meat and indulgent sauce pairs exceptionally well with sides like creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh Caesar Salad, offering a balanced and satisfying meal that's perfect for special occasions or a decadent dinner at home.

Ingredients

  • Steak - A prime cut of beef, known for its tenderness and rich flavor, is the star of this dish.
  • Salt and Black Pepper - These seasonings enhance the natural flavors of the steak and provide a well-balanced taste.
  • Olive Oil - Used for searing the steak, these oils help achieve a beautiful crust and enhance the cooking process.
  • Butter - The key to the luscious garlic butter sauce, adding richness and a silky texture to the dish.
  • Fresh Garlic Cloves - Minced garlic infuses the butter with a robust and aromatic flavor, elevating the overall taste.
  • Fresh Herbs - Herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley can be added as a garnish for an extra layer of fragrance and visual appeal to the steak.

Instructions

  1. Allow the steak to rest at room temperature, pat it dry, and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a skillet with oil until it shimmers but doesn't smoke.
  3. Place the seasoned steak in the hot pan and sear each side until desired doneness.
  4. Use tongs to flip the steak to the other side and cook further.
  5. Lower the heat, add butter and minced garlic, saut√© briefly without burning.
  6. Tilt the pan, baste the steak with garlic butter, and let it soak up the flavors.
  7. Remove and let the steak rest on a cutting board for 5-10 minutes.
  8. Sprinkle with fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley.
  9. Slice the rested steak and drizzle with remaining garlic butter sauce.
